## Research Summary

Mike King explains that Google AI Mode changes search from a relatively stable ranking system into a personalized and generative retrieval environment. Content is no longer competing only for a single keyword position. It must be relevant across multiple related queries, usable at the passage level, and suitable for inclusion in generated answers.

## Key Insights

1. AI Mode can expand one user query into multiple related synthetic queries through query fan-out.
2. Content may compete at the passage level rather than only as a complete page.
3. Semantic relevance, clarity, completeness, and topical alignment become critical selection factors.
4. Personalized user context can influence which sources and citations appear.
5. Text-only strategies are increasingly limited because AI search can use text, images, video, audio, and interactive formats.
6. Measurement should include visibility, citations, mentions, sentiment, and inclusion in generated answers rather than only rankings and clicks.

## Implications for B2B SaaS

B2B SaaS content teams should create interconnected content that covers customer problems, product use cases, comparisons, implementation questions, and related subtopics. AI-assisted workflows can help simulate query expansion, identify semantic gaps, improve passage clarity, and organize supporting content across multiple pages.

Content should also include strong product explanations, original evidence, clear definitions, useful examples, and multiple media formats. Human review remains necessary to ensure technical accuracy, positioning, and differentiation.
