## Research Summary

Lily Ray argues that GEO, AEO, LLMO, and AI Search Optimization are largely evolutions of established SEO and digital marketing practices rather than entirely separate disciplines. AI search changes how users ask questions, how information is summarized, and how visibility is measured, but it still depends heavily on accessible, trustworthy, structured, and authoritative web content.

## Key Insights

1. AI search expands the discovery ecosystem but does not eliminate traditional search.
2. Strong SEO foundations remain important because generative systems frequently retrieve information from search engines and public web sources.
3. Clear, self-contained answers are useful because AI systems may extract individual passages rather than evaluate only complete pages.
4. FAQs, question-and-answer formats, descriptive headings, tables, and structured content remain useful for both people and AI systems.
5. Brand reputation and third-party mentions can affect whether a company is recommended or cited.
6. Multimodal assets such as video, audio, images, and transcripts can strengthen discoverability.
7. New measurements include AI citations, share of voice, branded impressions, topic visibility, referral traffic, engagement, and conversions.
8. Citation visibility is volatile, so brands need ongoing monitoring rather than one-time optimization.

## Implications for B2B SaaS

B2B SaaS companies should clearly document their products, leadership, integrations, use cases, comparisons, customer questions, and technical concepts. Content should answer specific branded and non-branded questions in concise language while maintaining strong technical SEO and trustworthy supporting evidence.

AI-assisted content workflows can help discover question patterns, identify missing use-case pages, create initial structures, and monitor topic coverage. Human experts should validate claims, product positioning, examples, and final quality.
