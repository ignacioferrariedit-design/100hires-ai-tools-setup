## Research Summary

Aleyda Solís compares traditional SEO with AI Search Optimization, also described as GEO. Her framework shows that the two disciplines substantially overlap, but differ most clearly in user search behavior, optimization targets, goals, and measurement.

Traditional search commonly begins with shorter queries and leads users toward ranked pages. AI search often involves longer, conversational, and iterative prompts, with the platform synthesizing information into a direct response.

## Key Insights

1. Traditional SEO and AI Search Optimization share many technical and content-quality foundations.
2. The largest difference is how users formulate questions and interact with the results.
3. AI search optimization requires content that can be understood, extracted, summarized, and cited.
4. Measurement should extend beyond rankings and clicks to include citations, mentions, answer visibility, and influence within generated responses.
5. Existing SEO work remains valuable, but content teams should adapt structure and measurement for conversational discovery.

## Implications for B2B SaaS

B2B SaaS content systems should create content that addresses detailed customer questions, explains product use cases clearly, uses consistent terminology, and connects related concepts. AI-assisted workflows can help identify conversational queries and content gaps, but subject-matter experts should validate the final information.
